A little bit about my professional background: I graduated from George Washington University with a B.A. in Economics back in 2015. Following my graduation, I worked briefly as a communications analyst for a non-profit PAC in Washington, D.C., and subsequently took a job at a management consulting firm as an analyst on federeal healthcare and Veteran Affairs contracts. You can read more about my work as a management consultant at the bottom of my portfolio.

Now for the stuff you really care about! I started transitioning into web development early in 2017, first by taking courses on Lynda.com, and I eventually enrolled in a full-time web development bootcamp with Thinkful. My love for the web had been with me since I was a child, when I learned how to do basic HTML on forums I frequented. As I grew up, so did technology, and the evolution of tech keeps this passion thriving. While it took me a long time to finally realize it, web and movile technology is my greatest passion, and working as a developer is what I was always meant to do.

Since I started my more focused studies in development, I've learned how to be successful as a full-stack developer, using libraries and frameworks such as React, Redux, React Native, NodeJS, Express, MongoDB, and jQuery. While I lean more toward the front-end of development, I still have the full-stack experience to supplement my work.
